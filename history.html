History Of India

About:

  1. The history of India spans millennia, beginning with the urban around 2500 BCE. This gave way to the Vedic Period, laying foundational philosophies of Hinduism, Buddhism, and the caste system. Subsequent empires, medieval Mughal rule, and the British Raj ultimately led to independence in 1947.
  2. Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama was the first European to reach India by sea. He landed in Calicut (modern-day Kozhikode, Kerala) on May 20, 1498. This historic voyage inaugurated the era of European colonialism in India, opening direct maritime trade for lucrative spices like pepper and cinnamon.
  3. The title "Father of Indian History" is typically attributed to Megasthenes. He was a Greek ambassador and historian who documented ancient Indian society and governance in his work Indica during the Mauryan Empire.
  4. Chandragupta Maurya founded the Maurya Empire in 321 BCE. As the first to unify almost the entire Indian subcontinent under a single, centralized administration, he is widely regarded by historians as the first legitimate emperor of India.
  5. The word India comes from the Indus River, which was called Sindhu in Sanskrit. Persian invaders pronounced it as Hindu, and the Greeks later referred to the region as Indos or India. In contrast, Bharat is deeply rooted in indigenous history and mythology.
